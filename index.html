"DIGITAL ADVERTISING IS DEAD"

BY CHRIS GADEK
Gadek descibes the digital advertising industry is a horror story filled with repulsive abuse and privacy violations. He speculates digital advertising as dead and responds to it as "good riddance".
DIGITAL ADVERTISING 1994 TO 2023
Since the first advertisement banner in 1994, digital advertisers grew to understand that their most effective asset would be the curiosity of viewers.
From pop up ads spreading across the internet like wildfire, platforms blew up with ads consisting of "gossip", "health advice" and "celeb news" of celebrities you've never heard of in your life.
As we look closer to 2010, companies focused onto tracking and identity analysis to find profiles of individuals whom could serve as the perfect candidates for specific advertisers. With growing surveillance and profiling, privacy awareness spread among the consumers in 2010. This resulted in a political change due to the situation which could no longer be ignored.
